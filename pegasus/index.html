project pegasus

Pegasus is a web3 application that generates an NFT for each digitally verifiable task (or set of) that an individual completes, proving the value they have added to the world.

problem descriptor

Growing demand for jobs in companies that are growing, paired with a limited supply, leads to increasingly standardized testing processes in recruiting that are long, repetitive and generate no value.

the problem

With agencies like Randstad and LinkedIn connecting people to enterprises without extensively verifying their expertise and experience, the hiring processes are gradually inching towards verifing skills of candidates in an extremely standardized fashion. This problem exists in any industry that demands its employees to take an objective (especially determinate) test in the selection process to obtain proof of the candiate’s knowledge and problem-solving skills. This leads to time-consuming and repetitive hiring processes with a high probability of zero value generation on either side, since most candidates are rejected. The nature of humanity exacerbates this; when presented with a usually hard standardized test, people invest large amounts of time learning to beat it rather than learning from a larger pool of knowledge.

the solution

The utility of NFTs are widespread; they can conceptually be used to provide proof of knowledge and/or expertise. I plan to create a web3 app that generates non-tradeable NFTs for any digitally verifiable task that is successfully completed; these would represent the individual’s skill and knowledge in completing that task. A collection of these NFTs can be an individual’s digital portfolio that can be dynamically generated as they create. Unlike conventional portfolios, the claims here will be verifiable. Consequently, enterprises across many industries will no longer require standardized testing since the NFTs of a candidate are the proof of their knowledge and skill. This eliminates the need for initial skill verification testing, shortens hiring processes considerably, and provides employers much more data about claims on an NFT portfolio. People will no longer have to worry about investing large amounts of time to prepare for a multitude of hiring tests. If NFT collections unique to companies are distributed among its employees, all of an employee’s work under that company can be encoded under this one, and the NFT’s defintion can be extended to an employment contract, with attached benefits like health insurance, visa sponsorships, taxation records, etc.

proof of market

Over the summer, I reached out to fifty startup founders and CEOs across the world, from the Y-Combinator batches of S22 and S21, asking them if they found utility in a product that would give them proof of their employee candidates’ expertise/skill. Thirty-four of them were open to trying out the application. Ten of them wanted a demo and/or were keen on using the product right away. The eagerness of these companies from surveys is the only tangible evidence I have that it can be monetized in several ways (eg: a premium for more data about candidates’ experience). I have also asked a plethora of students at UWaterloo and prospective and present employees about using this, and not one person has said that they would not use it!
